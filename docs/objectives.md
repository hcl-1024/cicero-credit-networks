# Research Objectives and Interpretation

## O1 - Actor centrality by social role

O1 builds transaction-safe actor graphs and compares centrality by actor type. It asks whether family members, agents, freedmen, financiers, civic bodies, and other non-principal actors occupy important positions. Centrality measures documentary prominence within the surviving evidence, not objective historical importance.

## O2 - Dependence on Cicero's archive

O2 compares matched graph scopes with Cicero present and removed. It measures how much the reconstructed network depends on Cicero as both an actor and an archival vantage point. It does not reconstruct an archive-independent Roman financial network.

## O3 - Intermediaries and third parties

O3 separates direct borrower-lender relations from recommendation, surety, household management, delegated payment, enforcement, and other third-party relations. A third-party edge need not imply legal control of the obligation.

## O4 - Credit mechanisms and financial pressure

O4 classifies record-level and chain-level signals of refinancing, repayment pressure, account liquidity management, surety, and third-party dependence. These are transparent analytical classifications, not diagnoses of insolvency.

## O5 - Active private-credit stock in Cicero's community

O5 converts reviewed borrower-creditor exposure groups into active stock. Repeated mentions collapse; replacement or refinancing relations normally use a maximum or time-weighted exposure; public and context-only evidence is excluded from strict private stock. The graph-respecting route adds a controlled expected missing-edge increment to a reviewed register baseline.

## O5-6 - Cicero borrower-side distribution route

O5-6 uses nine approved high-confidence 45-44 BCE borrower-side components for Cicero. It then asks what a 600-senator comparison would look like if Cicero occupied selected positions in illustrative positive-stock distributions. These distributions are scenario families, not distributions fitted from the letters.

## O6 - Wider Roman-elite comparison

O6 scales the selected graph-respecting O5 stock through a class-weighted effective-population denominator to a 600-senator comparison. It is a transparent comparison exercise, not an observed total and not an estimate of aggregate elite wealth.

Machine-readable labels and questions are in `config/objectives.yml`.
